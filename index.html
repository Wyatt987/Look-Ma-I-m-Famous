Ferdinand Porsche

The Founder of Porsches
Early Life
Ferdinand Porsche was born on September 3, 1875, in Maffersdorf, Bohemia (now Vratislavice nad Nisou, Czech Republic). Fascinated by electricity from a young age, he helped his father during the day and studied at night. At 18, Porsche moved to Vienna to work for Béla Egger & Co. and studied part-time at the Vienna University of Technology, though he never earned a degree. In 1897, he built an electric wheel-hub motor and joined Hofwagenfabrik Jacob Lohner & Co., creating the P1 electric vehicle in 1898. His work gained global recognition at the 1900 Paris World’s Fair. After working for Austro-Daimler and Daimler-Motoren-Gesellschaft, he helped develop the Mercedes compressor car and was awarded numerous honors, including an honorary doctorate in 1917.
